FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Is it street legal?

Yes. This vehicle has a clean Texas title and can be registered for on-road use. It is currently titled and street legal in the state of Texas.

Can I register it in my state?

In most U.S. states, yes. A clean Texas title transfers to other states without issue. However, California and a few other states have strict emissions requirements — check your local CARB/emissions rules before purchasing. Some states may also have width or lighting requirements.

What does TMU mean?

TMU stands for Total Mileage Unknown. The odometer reads 742 miles, but military vehicles frequently have odometers replaced or reset during service. The reading cannot be verified as actual lifetime mileage. This is standard for virtually all military surplus vehicles.

How fast does it go?

Top speed is approximately 55–65 MPH depending on gearing, load, and terrain. These vehicles were designed for off-road durability, not highway speed. Highway driving is possible but not ideal for long distances.

What fuel does it use?

Standard commercial diesel fuel. The 6.5L was designed as a multi-fuel engine and will run on JP-8, DF-2, or regular pump diesel. The tank holds 25 gallons and expect roughly 8–12 MPG.

Is the 6.5L turbo-able?

Yes. The 6.5L NA diesel is commonly upgraded with a turbo kit. This is one of the most popular aftermarket modifications for HMMWVs. The engine and transmission in this vehicle have been rebuilt, providing a solid foundation for upgrades.

What about parts availability?

Parts availability is excellent. Military surplus parts are widely available through government surplus channels, and aftermarket support for HMMWVs continues to grow. Common wear items, drivetrain components, and body panels are all readily sourced.

Does it have a 12V or 24V electrical system?

The HMMWV uses a 24V DC system with dual 12V batteries wired in series. Standard 12V accessories (radios, lights, chargers) will require a 24V-to-12V converter or a dedicated 12V tap from one battery. Conversion kits are available.

Will it fit in my garage?

Probably not. The HMMWV is 86.5 inches wide (over 7 feet) — wider than a standard garage door opening and most parking spaces. Plan for outdoor storage or an oversized garage.
